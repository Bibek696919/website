Early Life

Messi was born in Rosario, Argentina, and showed an early talent for football. He started playing football at the age of 5 with a local club called Grandoli, where his father worked as a steel factory manager. By the age of 8, Messi joined Newell’s Old Boys, a local football club. It was clear from the start that Messi had exceptional talent, but his career almost took a different path due to a serious setback.

Most of the children would grow normally but he was diagnosed with growth hormone deficiency. That led to a certain sadness. But At just 11 years old,Messi moved from Argentina to Spain to join FC Barcelona's youth academy, La Masia, after the club agreed to cover his medical expenses.

Youth Carrer

Newell's Old Boys

Messi is a lifelong supporter of Newell's Old Boys, based in Rosario. When he was seven years old, he first started playing for Newell's. During the six years he played for them, he scored almost 500 goals, and was a member of "The Machine of '87", a nearly unbeatable youth side named for the year of their birth. Messi would regularly entertain crowds by performing ball tricks during half-time, when the first team played home games. His goalscoring idol growing up was Brazilian striker Ronaldo, who Messi called "the best forward I've ever seen".

Professional Career

Messi’s professional career began when he debuted for FC Barcelona’s senior team in 2004 at just 17. From that moment, he evolved into the club’s greatest player, shaping an era defined by extraordinary skill, consistency, and unmatched creativity. Over 17 seasons with Barcelona, Messi became their all-time top scorer and the centerpiece of a team that won numerous La Liga titles, domestic cups, and four UEFA Champions League trophies. His dribbling, vision, and goal-scoring ability transformed modern football standards.
In 2021, financial issues at Barcelona forced his emotional departure, leading him to Paris Saint-Germain, where he added more titles and continued showcasing his brilliance. In 2023, he began a new chapter with Inter Miami in Major League Soccer, raising the league’s global profile. Parallel to his club success, Messi reached the ultimate peak by leading Argentina to victory in the 2022 FIFA World Cup, solidifying his legacy as one of football’s greatest icons.

International Career

Lionel Messi’s international career with Argentina began in 2005, when he made his senior debut as a teenager. Though he displayed incredible talent early on, major trophies initially eluded him, and several painful losses—including the 2014 World Cup final and multiple Copa América finals—led to immense pressure and even a brief retirement from the national team in 2016. However, Messi returned with renewed determination, becoming the emotional and tactical leader of Argentina.
His breakthrough came in 2021, when he guided Argentina to victory in the Copa América, ending a 28-year trophy drought and earning Player of the Tournament. This triumph set the stage for his greatest achievement: captaining Argentina to win the 2022 FIFA World Cup in Qatar. His magical performances, leadership, and decisive goals defined the tournament. Today, Messi stands as Argentina’s all-time top scorer and a national hero, having completed one of football’s most inspiring international journeys.

Style Of Play

Lionel Messi’s style of play is defined by his exceptional dribbling, vision, and playmaking abilities. Low center of gravity and close ball control allow him to glide past defenders with ease, even at high speed. Messi is known for his quick, precise touches, often leaving opponents behind with a series of feints and sudden bursts of acceleration. He is a brilliant playmaker, capable of delivering perfect passes in tight spaces, creating opportunities from almost any position on the field. His vision and decision-making are unparalleled, as he consistently reads the game two steps ahead. Messi’s goal-scoring prowess is remarkable, with a lethal left foot that can finish from almost anywhere—whether it’s a curling free kick, a clinical shot inside the box, or a long-range strike. Versatile in attack, he seamlessly shifts between a winger, forward, or even a deeper playmaker, making him a constant threat to opposition defenses.
